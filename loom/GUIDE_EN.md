# Loom — User Guide
### For users · en

---

## What is Loom

Loom is the memory of your dreams.

You have been recording dreams for years — in different journals, apps, notebooks. Loom connects all of it and helps you see what you may have missed: places that keep returning in your dreams, figures that appear again and again, emotions that repeat in different forms.

Loom does not interpret your dreams. It does not know what they mean. It simply notices that certain things recur — and shows you that.

---

## How it works

When you add a dream to any app (Dream Browser, Lucid Lab, Oneiro), Loom:

1. **Reads** the dream text
2. **Understands** meaning — not literally, but semantically. "Staro mesto" and "old town" are the same thing to it.
3. **Connects** it to past dreams describing similar experiences
4. **Notices** patterns that repeat across time

All of this happens in the background. You just keep recording dreams as you always have.

## What Loom can do

### Search your archive

Type a word or phrase — Loom finds all dreams describing a similar experience, regardless of when you wrote them or in which language.

```
Searching: "old town"

Results:
  [87%] 2019-03-12  Sprehajam se po starem delu mesta...
  [81%] 2021-07-04  Ulice so bile kamnite in ozke...
  [76%] 2023-11-18  Ancient city with narrow streets...
```

Search works in Slovenian and English — same results either way.

### Similar dreams

For any dream, Loom shows you which other dreams are most similar in experience — not by words, but by meaning.

### Recurring patterns *(coming soon)*

Over time, Loom will notice that certain themes keep appearing: searching, falling, fleeing, unknown spaces. It will suggest these patterns for your confirmation — you decide whether they feel meaningful.

### Emotional geography *(coming soon)*

Places in your dreams will accumulate their own history: how often they appear, what emotions they carry, how they have changed across the years.

## Privacy

Loom runs locally on your computer. Your dreams go nowhere.

The only exception is the generation of semantic vectors — for this, Loom sends dream text to a Hugging Face server. The text is not stored there, only processed and returned as a mathematical vector. The original text cannot be reconstructed from that vector.

A fully local, offline mode will be available in the future.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Loom interpret my dreams?**
No. Loom notices patterns — you interpret them. Loom never claims to know what your dreams mean.

**Do I need to change how I record dreams?**
No. Keep recording as you always have, in whichever app you use. Loom reads existing data.

**What about old dreams?**
Loom retroactively processes your entire archive — including dreams from 20 years ago. This is one of its strongest features.

**Does it work in Slovenian?**
Yes. Loom is designed for both Slovenian and English. Both languages work equally well.

**How many dreams does Loom need?**
Patterns become visible at around 50 dreams. The larger the archive, the better the results. With 1000+ dreams, Loom begins to notice subtle long-term patterns.

**Do I need to be connected to the internet all the time?**
Not for searching and reading. For generating embeddings for new dreams, yes — until the local mode becomes available.

## What Loom is not

Loom is not a therapeutic tool. It does not replace professional support.

Loom is not an oracle. It does not know what your dreams mean.

Loom is not surveillance. It does not share your data, sell information, or build advertising profiles.

Loom is simply — memory. Yours, local, transparent.
